Concept: Group Discussion - Disagreeing Politely
Level: A1
Lesson type: Can Do

GD topics to use for sentences: Online Shopping vs. Offline Shopping, Social Media — Boon or Bane, Work From Home vs. Work From Office, Traditional Learning vs. Online Learning, Internship — Boon or Bane, Gender Equality, Face-to-Face vs. Virtual Communication, Women Development in India

Sentence structure:
"I see your point, but I think [alternative view]."

Pre and Post Questions:
1. "I see your point, but I think online shopping has delivery issues."
2. "I see your point, but I think social media can be used well."
3. "I see your point, but I think work from home needs self-control."
4. "I see your point, but I think classrooms keep students on track."
5. "I see your point, but I think internships take time from studies."
6. "I see your point, but I think gender equality needs more action."
7. "I see your point, but I think video calls miss real connection."
8. "I see your point, but I think classroom teaching helps more."
9. "I see your point, but I think women deserve equal chances."
10. "I see your point, but I think stores give better service."
11. "I see your point, but I think online classes feel lonely."
12. "I see your point, but I think office routine builds habits."
13. "I see your point, but I think social media spreads fake news too."
14. "I see your point, but I think both options have their place."
15. "I see your point, but I think interns should get paid."
16. "I see your point, but I think virtual calls save a lot of time."
17. "I see your point, but I think classroom discussions are richer."
18. "I see your point, but I think remote work suits many people."
19. "I see your point, but I think things are slowly improving."
20. "I see your point, but I think buying in stores feels safer."
