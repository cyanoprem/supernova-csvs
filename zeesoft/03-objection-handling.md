Concept: Domain-Specific Communication - Objection Handling
Level: A1
Lesson type: Can Do

Scenarios to use for sentences: Client pushback on pricing, Stakeholder doubts about a proposed timeline, Team disagreement on technology choice, Manager questioning resource allocation, Customer complaint about product quality, Vendor negotiation pushback, Prospect hesitant about switching from a competitor

---

Sub-concept 1: Acknowledging the concern
Description: The learner can accept someone's objection respectfully before responding
Sentence structures:
1. "I understand your concern about [issue]."
   Example: "I understand your concern about the pricing."
2. "That's a fair point. Let me explain why we [action]."
   Example: "That's a fair point. Let me explain why we picked this tool."
3. "Thanks for raising this. [Reason it matters]."
   Example: "Thanks for raising this. Timelines are important for your launch."

---

Sub-concept 2: Giving a different perspective
Description: The learner can offer another way to look at the situation
Sentence structures:
1. "I see your point, but from [angle], [reframed point]."
   Example: "I see your point, but from a long-term view, this saves money."
2. "We suggested [option] because [benefit]."
   Example: "We suggested the yearly plan because it costs 30% less per month."
3. "Other clients in [field] found that [result]."
   Example: "Other clients in edtech found that good onboarding reduces drop-offs."

---

Sub-concept 3: Offering a way forward
Description: The learner can suggest a next step to move past the objection
Sentence structures:
1. "How about we [compromise] first?"
   Example: "How about we try a 3-month pilot first?"
2. "I can send you [item] by [day] to help you decide."
   Example: "I can send you a comparison report by Monday to help you decide."
3. "If [option] works for you, we can start with that."
   Example: "If a smaller rollout works for you, we can start with that."
