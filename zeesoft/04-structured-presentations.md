Concept: Domain-Specific Communication - Structured Presentations
Level: A1
Lesson type: Can Do

Scenarios to use for sentences: Product pitch to investors, Team project presentation, College seminar or viva, Company quarterly review, Training session delivery, Proposal presentation to a client, Conference talk or webinar

---

Sub-concept 1: Opening the presentation
Description: The learner can introduce themselves and the topic clearly
Sentence structures:
1. "Hi, I'm [name] from [team]. Today I'll talk about [topic]."
   Example: "Hi, I'm Ananya from the product team. Today I'll talk about our new onboarding flow."
2. "Let me start with a question — [question]?"
   Example: "Let me start with a question — have you ever left a sign-up page because it was too long?"
3. "By the end, you'll know [takeaway]."
   Example: "By the end, you'll know our plan to reduce churn by 25%."

---

Sub-concept 2: Moving between points
Description: The learner can organize key points and switch between sections smoothly
Sentence structures:
1. "I'll cover [number] things — [point 1], [point 2], and [point 3]."
   Example: "I'll cover three things — the problem, our solution, and the results."
2. "Now let's move to [next point]."
   Example: "Now let's move to our go-to-market plan."
3. "The main point here is [insight]. This links to [next topic]."
   Example: "The main point here is that 60% of users drop off at checkout. This links to our redesign plan."

---

Sub-concept 3: Closing and taking questions
Description: The learner can wrap up and handle audience questions
Sentence structures:
1. "To sum up — [point 1], [point 2], and [point 3]."
   Example: "To sum up — we have product-market fit, strong numbers, and we're ready to grow."
2. "Thank you. Happy to take questions."
   Example: "Thank you. Happy to take questions."
3. "Good question. [Short answer]."
   Example: "Good question. We chose AWS because it was cheaper for our size."
